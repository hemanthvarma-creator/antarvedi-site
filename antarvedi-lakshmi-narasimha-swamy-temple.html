Sri Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy Temple

A sacred pilgrimage site at the confluence of the Godavari River and the Bay of Bengal

Book Darshan →

Sri Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy Temple, Antarvedi, is an ancient and revered shrine located at the sacred confluence of the Godavari River and the Bay of Bengal. Established by Sage Vasishta, the temple attracts thousands of devotees and is traditionally known as Dakshina Kashi — the Kashi of South India.

History & Sacred Traditions

Sri Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy Temple at Antarvedi is one of the most historically significant Vaishnavite temples in Andhra Pradesh. Located at the divine confluence of the Vasishta branch of the Godavari River and the Bay of Bengal, this holy place is revered as a powerful kshetram where river, sea, and spirituality unite.

The presiding deity, Lord Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy, represents the fierce yet compassionate form of Lord Vishnu, symbolizing protection of devotees and the destruction of adharma. Devotees believe that worshipping Lord Narasimha at Antarvedi grants courage, relief from fear, spiritual strength, and divine blessings for generations.

According to historical records and temple inscriptions, the temple was rebuilt in the early 19th century by Sri Koppanathi Krishnamma, a devotee of Lord Vishnu from the Agni Kula Kshatriya community, with major renovations completed around 1823 AD (Shalivahana Saka 1745).

Antarvedi finds mention in local Puranic lore. The great sage Vasishtha performed intense penance at this sacred confluence. In the Treta Yuga, Lord Rama is believed to have visited during his exile to worship Lord Narasimha. In the Dwapara Yuga, Arjuna is said to have meditated here to gain spiritual clarity and warrior strength.

The Antarvedi Theertham — a holy dip where river meets sea — is believed to wash away sins and grant moksha. Pilgrims gather for the grand Sri Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy Vari Kalyanam on Bheeshma Ekadasi, followed by the magnificent Rathotsavam the next day.

Major festivals including Bheeshma Ekadasi, Ratha Saptami, Narasimha Jayanti, and the divine Tirukalyanam are celebrated with immense devotion, drawing pilgrims from across Andhra Pradesh and neighboring states.
