Sagara
Sangamam

Where the sacred Godavari River merges into the vast Bay of Bengal

Sangamam at Antarvedi is a sacred confluence where the holy River Godavari merges with the Bay of Bengal. This rare natural phenomenon holds immense spiritual significance and creates a serene yet powerful atmosphere — symbolizing unity, purification, and divine balance.

Spiritual & Natural Significance

🙏 Spiritual Importance

  • Considered highly sacred in Hindu tradition
  • Devotees perform holy snanam (ritual bath)
  • Pitru tarpana rituals are commonly observed
  • Closely associated with Antarvedi Temple worship

🌊 Natural Beauty

  • Visible contrast between river and sea waters
  • Peaceful open horizon, unlike any other place
  • Ideal for sunrise and early morning meditation
  • Extraordinary photography location

🗓️ Best Time to Visit

  • Season: October to March
  • Time: Early morning & at sunset
  • Monsoon months may have strong currents
  • Bheeshma Ekadasi — most sacred day

Note: Swimming near the confluence is not recommended due to strong undercurrents. Visitors are advised to follow local guidance, respect religious practices, and preserve the natural environment.
